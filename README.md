# Mechanics Revisited

A novice-first mechanics curriculum built and audited one chapter at a time.
The deck is displayed under `misc` so it can be compared with the existing
mechanics deck without replacing it.

## Scope

- Subject: `misc` (physics authoring standard)
- Learner level: zero mechanics knowledge
- Confirmed mathematical/tool prerequisites: arithmetic; fractions and ratios;
  powers of ten; substitution into a simple formula; reading a number line
- Confirmed subject prerequisites: none
- What mastery enables: describing and predicting the behavior of everyday
  macroscopic objects with progressively richer mechanical models
- Deliberate exclusions: relativity, quantum mechanics, continuum mechanics,
  fluid mechanics, and advanced calculus-based derivations

The pilot assumes ordinary words such as *object*, *place*, *change*, and
*time*. It does not assume that the learner knows physics meanings of terms
such as velocity, acceleration, force, energy, momentum, or field.

## Chapter map

| File | Topic | Prerequisites | Learning outcomes |
|---|---|---|---|
| `01_foundations.md` | Measurement, units, uncertainty, dimensions, estimation | Confirmed mathematics above | Report and compare measurements; convert units; reason about resolution and uncertainty; check dimensions; estimate scale |
| `02_vectors.md` | Directional quantities and vector representations | Chapter 1 | Distinguish scalar and directional descriptions; translate among words, arrows, and components |
| `03_kinematics_1d.md` | Describing motion along a line | Chapters 1–2 | Define position, displacement, velocity, and acceleration; read and relate motion graphs |
| `04_kinematics_2d.md` | Motion in a plane | Chapters 1–3 | Decompose and reconstruct planar motion; analyze projectile and relative motion |
| `05_newtons_laws.md` | Interactions and Newton's laws | Chapters 1–4 | Build interaction models; connect net force to changes in motion |
| `06_forces.md` | Common force models | Chapter 5 | Choose and apply gravitational, normal, tension, friction, and drag models |
| `07_work_energy.md` | Work and energy accounting | Chapters 1–6 | Relate work to energy change; construct and evaluate energy-accounting models |
| `08_momentum.md` | Impulse, momentum, and collisions | Chapters 1–6 | Relate impulse to momentum change; analyze isolated-system collisions |
| `09_rotation.md` | Rotational kinematics and dynamics | Chapters 1–8 | Translate linear ideas to rotation; reason with torque and angular momentum |
| `10_gravitation.md` | Universal gravitation and orbits | Chapters 1–9 | Apply the inverse-square gravitational model to fields, energy, and orbits |
| `11_oscillations.md` | Periodic motion | Chapters 1–10 | Identify restoring behavior; connect oscillation representations and parameters |
| `12_synthesis.md` | Model selection and multi-step problems | Chapters 1–11 | Select, combine, test, and communicate mechanics models across contexts |

Planned outcomes in later rows are not prerequisites for earlier rows. Only
`01_foundations.md` is authored in this pilot; later chapter files intentionally
remain empty until the pilot has been learned and evaluated.
